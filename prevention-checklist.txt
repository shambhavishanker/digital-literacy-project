Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for College Students 

1. UPI PINs are only for sending money: you never need to enter your PIN to receive cash. If a buyer asks you to scan a code and enter your PIN, it's a scam. 

2. Watch out for scam: If a stranger randomly sends a small amount to your GPay, claims it was an accident, and pressures you to return a larger amount, don't do it. It's a trap. 

3. Don't click random panic links: If you get an urgent SMS saying your electricity bill is due or your bank account is about to be blocked, ignore it. 

4. Never download remote-access apps: Scammers will tell you to install apps like "Team connect" to help you fix a banking issue, but it just gives them full control of your phone. 

5. Stop and verify: If you get a terrifying call saying you're under "digital arrest" for a package you didn't send, hang up and call official authorities directly. 

6. Double-check the receiver's name: A tiny typo in a UPI ID can send your money to a completely random person, and you probably won't be able to undo it. 

7. Keep your phone updated: Just install your OS updates and use an antivirus so malware can't easily hide in fake apps. 

8. Report it immediately: If you get scammed, freeze your account ASAP. Call the Helpline at 1930 or report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) immediately to improve your chances of getting the money back.
